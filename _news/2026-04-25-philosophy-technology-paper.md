Our paper **"A Conceptual Crosswalk Between Trustworthy Records and Trustworthy AI"** by Lais Barbudo Carrasco, Tove Engvall, and Felix Dobslaw has been accepted for publication in *Philosophy & Technology* (Springer Nature).

The paper bridges archival science and AI: where archival science has long grounded trustworthiness in authenticity, integrity, and provenance of records, Trustworthy AI frameworks tend to reduce it to a technical checklist of transparency and explainability. Drawing on a scoping review of 151 papers and a conceptual crosswalk between the two fields, we argue that integrating archival principles reframes AI trustworthiness as an ongoing institutional and ethical commitment — not just a system-level property.
